Project summary

This project poses a comparative analysis of the stock price behaviour and volatility of HDFC Bank and Axis Bank during the period 2001 to 2021 by the utilisation of Python and Pandas library. Both the institutions conflicts are outstanding private sector financial institution in India and taking an important role in national banking system. The key objective of this study is to scrutinise long-term trends, return characteristics and volatility patterns so as to gain a better understanding of their historical performance and accompanying risk profiles.

The analytical procedure starts with ingestion of historical stock price data for both the banks followed by methodical cleaning and preprocessing of the data. This process includes management of missing values, conversion of date fields to proper datetime types, standardisation of column names, and checking of numerical consistency between key financial variables such as opening price, closing price and trading volume. Daily returns are then calculated in order to be compared in a more subtle way between the two securities in terms of price movement and volatility.

Exploratory data analysis techniques are used in order to decipher the trends and pattern across time. Line plots are created to graph long-tailed closing prices as well as to highlight the paths of growth and big market fluctuations. Histograms and distribution plots of daily returns are utilised to juxtapose the nature of volatility and the dispersion of returns between the two banks. These kinds of visualisations provide information on periods of increased risk and relative calm.

On the whole, the project shows the successful use of Python and Pandas for the analysis of financial data including the implementation of data wrangling, data transformation, and data visualisation techniques. The findings add to the better understanding of comparative stock performance in the Indian banking industry and highlight the importance that can be derived from historical market data to inform financial analysis and decision-making.

Data Visualization

Conclusion

This project attempted a comparative study of the stock price behaviour and volatility (HDFC Bank and Axis Bank) of these banks between the year 2001 to 2021 using Python-based data analysis techniques. By conducting a series of systematic cleaning operations, transformations, and exploratory analyses, the research was able to show convergences as well as divergences in the long-term performance paths of the two banking institutions. The depiction of closing price trends behaved as long-term growth for both these entities while concurrently absorbed dynamic variations in the market at episodes of economic stress and financial ambiguity.

The analysis of daily returns made possible to gain some insight into patterns of volatility, showing how the price varied over time and between the two stocks. Distributional plots showed variations in the dispersion of the returns hence representing different risk characteristics associated with the different banks. These results highlight the importance of analysing returns instead of absolute price levels in the assessment of financial risk.

Collectively the project provides an example of how well Pandas and Matplotlib work to perform the operations on real-world financial data and retrieve meaning from data. Although this analysis is limited by a focus on past data and does not consider the influence of external macroeconomic variables or firm-specific variables it provides a sound basis to enable further investigation. Prospective research can further expand this research by considering the other banking entities, using advanced risk metrics, or implementing predictive modelling techniques to gain more insight into the dynamics of the stock market in the Indian banking sector.
